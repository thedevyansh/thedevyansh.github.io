Minimalism ✨

"The secret of happiness is not found in seeking more, but in developing the capacity to enjoy less." Socrates



Alright! Let's get into it. Minimalism isn't something which is restricted to a particular domain, genre, or scenario. It is, in general, related to almost anything we can think of. Having said that, knowing what it infers tangibly is quite consequential, for the odds are astronomical.
We'll see how!

Minimalism, in layman's terms, means simplicity (or less clutter). However, it could take an individual quite sometime to understand the complexity involved, making the concept arcane. It requires the power of perceiving, and experience. The great thing about minimalism, however, is the fact that anyone can relate to what it has to offer, after a bit of practice.

THE RATIONALE:

When there is no desire, all things are at peace, because a good life is birthed from great habits. The habits which consolidate our mind, the habits which regard the journey as the reward. And the habits which disregard the conventional mentality of "having more, to live more." Being able to appreciate that price of a thing isn't the same as the value of a thing, is a path to minimalism. This facilitates building a lifestyle around life, and not style.
It is conspicuous that less clutter means more space for what matter. Minimalism, thus implies: the more you give up, the more you gain (which could be unintuitive at the first glance). People who follow minimalism dearly reckons that buying less is great, but wanting less is better, because quality is utmost, and experiences are greater than things.

Thus, by giving up on things that do not matter, and seeking progress, not perfection, can really go (and they do!) a long way to uplift one's personality, eloquency, and mental sanity.
The objective should be a meaningful life, and not happiness. After all, the willingness and ability to let go is the most notable and sensible demeanour one can possess.
